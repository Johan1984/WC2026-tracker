Per-Match Form — opponent-adjusted xG (optional but recommended)

For each of the last 10 games enter the opponent's Elo (eloratings.net), the team's xG created (xGF) and xG conceded (xGA) in that match — read straight off fbref.com or sofascore.com. xG, not the actual scoreline, because it strips out luck and finishing variance and predicts future goals better. The model then rates each game against what an average team would have managed versus that same opponent, so 1.8 xG against a 1400-rated side counts for far less than 1.8 xG against a 2000-rated side. Fill any number of rows (blanks ignored); one filled row overrides the quick-mode averages above. If a match has no xG available, you can enter actual goals instead — same scale, just noisier.

This is your real scoreboard, not the bankroll. CLV (closing line value) measures whether you keep buying prices the market later agrees were too cheap. A handful of wins or losses is mostly luck; average CLV turns positive long before profit does if your model is genuinely sharp. Aim for positive average CLV across 50+ placed bets. If it's flat or negative after that, the model has no edge on these markets — no staking trick fixes that.

Closing Line Value
—
Closing odds = the price for this exact market the moment the match kicked off. You don't need to be awake — look it up the next day on oddsportal.com (it archives opening & closing odds for finished matches). CLV = how much your taken price beat the close. Positive CLV over many bets is the single strongest sign your model has a real edge — far more reliable than your win/loss record.
